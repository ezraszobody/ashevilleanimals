Cardinals

Very common around Asheville. The Female Cardinal is usually very brown with a hint of red in places. Whereas the male is a very vibrant red all over. One interesting fact is that they both fluff up their feathers during the winter to stay warm. Their legs and feet do not have feathers. Thus they are vulnerable to cold. When it is very cold, like other birds, cardinals will shiver to keep warm. Overall, the cardinal is a very beautiful and common bird, you can see them almost anywhere in the eastern United States.
